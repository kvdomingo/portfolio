For this activity, the CIE 1964 standard observer values for 380--780 nm was obtained from [[2](#CIE64)]. The
tristimulus values were calculated using [[1](#Soriano2019)]

<span id="eq-tristimulus" class="mx-auto">
$
\begin{align}
  X &= \int P(\lambda) \bar{x}(\lambda) \frac{\rm{d}}{\rm{d}\lambda} \nonumber \\
	Y &= \int P(\lambda) \bar{y}(\lambda) \frac{\rm{d}}{\rm{d}\lambda} \\
	Z &= \int P(\lambda) \bar{z}(\lambda) \frac{\rm{d}}{\rm{d}\lambda} \nonumber
\end{align}
$
</span>

Their $xy$ coordinates were obtained by normalizing each value by their sum. Figure&nbsp;[1](#fig-locus) shows the
obtained boundaries for the CIE tongue by considering monochromatic lights ($P(\lambda) = \delta(\lambda)$). The
Planckian locus was obtained by substituting into $P(\lambda)$ the spectral radiant exitance for a blackbody, given by

<span id="eq-planck" class="mx-auto">
$
\begin{equation}
  M(\lambda, T) = \frac{2\pi hc^2}{\lambda^5} \frac{1}{\exp\left[\frac{hc}{k\lambda T}\right] - 1}
\end{equation}
$
</span>
